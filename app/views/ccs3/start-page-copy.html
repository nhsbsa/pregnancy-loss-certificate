Request a baby loss certificate

If your pregnancy ends before 24 weeks, you can get a certificate in memory of your baby.

Parents do not have to request a certificate - it’s optional, its free to get a certificate.

Why we're contacting you

We've received a request for a baby loss certificate.

The main applicant has named you as the other parent of their baby. They would like to include your name on their certificate.

If you would like your name on their certificate, we'll ask you some questions.

The main applicant will need to give you a reference number.

If you choose not to add your name, the other parent can request a certificate that shows only themselves as the named parent.

About the certificate

The certificate is not a legal document. For example, you cannot use it to claim benefits.

It can show:

  • name of parents
  • parents' relationship to their baby
  • baby's name
  • baby's sex
  • date of baby loss
  • place of baby loss

The certificate will not be added to your GP record.

If parents have lost more than one pregnancy, they can apply for a separate certificate for each baby loss.

If your pregnancy ends from 24 weeks onwards, you need to register a stillbirth instead.

Your certificate should arrive in the post within 14 days. If you do not receive it, you can request another certificate.
