If your pregnancy ends before 24 weeks, you can get a certificate in memory of your baby.

You do not have to request a certificate - it’s optional.

The certificate is not a legal document. For example, you cannot use it to claim benefits.

The certificate will not be added to your GP record.

If your pregnancy ends from 24 weeks onwards, you need to register a stillbirth instead.

Request a certificate

You can ask for a certificate if all these apply:

  • you’ve lost a baby before the 24th week of pregnancy
  • you’re one of the baby’s parents or surrogate
  • you’re at least 16 years old
  • your pregnancy ended during the past 5 years
  • your pregnancy ended in England
  • you live in England

If you’ve lost more than one pregnancy, you can apply for a separate certificate for each baby loss.

Your certificate should arrive in the post within 14 days. If you do not receive it, you can request another certificate.

Before you start

You'll need:

  • your NHS number or postcode registered with your GP
  • access to your mobile phone or email address registered with your GP
  • permission from the other parent if you also want their name on the certificate
  • permission from the other parent and their email address if you also want their name on the certificate

If you’re including the other parent’s name on the certificate, you’ll need their date of birth and email address. They’ll have 7 days to confirm their details and give their permission.
