Request a baby loss certificate

If a pregnancy ends before 24 weeks (or 28 weeks if a pregnancy ended before 1 October 1992), parents can get a certificate in memory of their baby.

Parents do not have to request a certificate - it’s optional. It’s free to get a certificate.

Why we're contacting you

We've received a request for a baby loss certificate.

The main applicant has named you as the other parent of their baby. They would like to include your name on their certificate.

If you would like your name on their certificate, we’ll ask you some questions. You’ll have 7 days to give permission and confirm your details.

One certificate will be posted to the main applicant. If you would like your own certificate, you can request one.

The main applicant can still request a certificate that shows only their name as a parent of their baby if you do not:

  • respond within 7 days
  • give permission to be named on the certificate

About the certificate

The certificate is not a legal document. For example, it cannot be used to claim benefits.

It can show:

  • name of parents
  • relationship to baby
  • date of baby loss
  • place of baby loss
  • baby's sex
  • baby's name

The certificate will not be added to your GP record.

If parents have lost more than one pregnancy, they can request a separate certificate for each loss. This can be done straight after their first request or later.

Before you start

You'll need:

  • your NHS number or postcode registered with your GP
  • the mobile phone number or email address registered with your GP
  • the reference number in your email
